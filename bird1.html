Name:Baltimore Oriole

Species:Icterus galbula

Family:Icteridae

Habitat:Baltimore orioles are often found high up in large, leafy deciduous trees, but do not generally reside in deep forests.

Place of found:Nearctic,Canadian Prairies,Montana

Diet:insects, berries, and nectar

Description:The Baltimore oriole (Icterus galbula) is a small icterid blackbird common in eastern North America as a migratory breeding bird. It received its name from the resemblance of the male's colors to those on the coat-of-arms of 17th-century Lord Baltimore.

Weight:22.3 - 42 g

Height:17 - 22 cm
